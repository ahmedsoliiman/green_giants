Since the time of the pharaohs, Egypt has depended on agriculture and fishing. but, overtime agriculture has faced some problems, such as agricultural waste, which is considered one of the most dangerous types of problems and one of these problems is the common reed plant, It is one of the most important aquatic plants that is widely spread in Egypt.The common reed plant is distributed in most lakes, canals and many more places.It has the same characteristics as the water lily, which helps it to spread and adapt to the environment, and the ability to spread quickly. Despite all of this and the existence of the plant in a sustainable way, no one benefits from it. God gives us livelihood and treasure (but unfortunately, people get rid of it by burning it).This pollutes the environment due to the carbon dioxide and other gases that come out of it, and it also causes an increase in global warming. So as TEAM GG we were able to reach a solution and benefit from this plant and use it in many things that are very useful for the environment, such as the cellulose that we extracted from the common reed plant and we were able to extract from it a product of environmentally friendly water filters. We were also able to extract from the cellulose (lignin) from which we extracted gum, rubber, and liquid fertilizer. Can a plant like common reed solve this environmental pollution issue and help us with the rich resources it contains and open the door to a better future?

problem identification

The growth of common reed and its presence in inappropriate places such as the Nile and canals disrupts the movement of fishing boats in canals and rivers and cuts the fishermen's nets.This harms the fishermen's livelihood instead of making them earn for a living. and they are forced to get rid of it in ways that are harmful to the environment and waste resources.In mid-2012, an urgent meeting was held at the Ministry of International Cooperation in Cairo. The meeting was about the protests of fishermen in Mariout lake, specifically in the northwestern corner of the Nile Delta.

competitors

Alternative resources for cellulose and lignin: Companies that extract cellulose and lignin from other sources, such as wood pulp, could be considered competitors depending on the specific applications and cost effectiveness of using reed.

analysis

Cultivated area: Data shows that the cultivated area of ​​reed in Egypt ranges between 200 and 300 thousand acres. Productivity: Productivity ranges between 10 and 15 tons per acre. Consumption: Approximately 50% of the reed production is consumed locally, while the rest is exported. Exports: Egypt exports reed to many Arab and European countries. Imports: Egypt does not import reed in large quantities.

Methods for Extracting Nanocellulose from Cellulose

Cutting Unit:

This method relies on applying high shear forces to cellulose fibers to break them down into nanofibers. Advantages: Easy to apply. Low cost. Disadvantages: May damage the properties of nanocellulose. Difficulty in controlling the size and shape of nanocellulose. Acids and bases used to break down cellulose into nanofibers: Acids: Sulfuric acid: It is the most common acid used for extracting nanocellulose. Bases: Sodium hydroxide: It is the most common base used for extracting nanocellulose.

Methods for Purifying Lignin from Impurities

Filtration:

1- Coarse filtration:
A strainer with large holes is used to remove large objects such as seed pieces and leaves.

2- Fine filtration:
Cheesecloth or filter paper is used to remove small impurities such as sand and silt.

Washing:

*The lignin is washed with clean water to remove any dirt attached to it.

*A mild saline solution (1 teaspoon salt per cup of water) can be used to sterilize the lignin.

Settling:

The lignin is left in a calm container to settle. Heavy impurities will settle at the bottom of the container, while clean lignin will float on the surface.

Centrifugation:

* A centrifuge is used to separate lignin from impurities quickly and efficiently.

* The lignin sample is placed in centrifuge tubes, and the machine spins at high speed, causing the impurities to separate from the lignin.

Chemical Methods:

* Some chemicals are used to remove certain types of impurities, such as: *Activated carbon: It is used to remove unwanted odors and tastes. *Chlorine:It is used to sterilize lignin and kill bacteria.
